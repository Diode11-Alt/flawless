# Non-EU Nationals Working in the UAE: The Work Permit Process, Step by Step

Hiring non-EU talent (Third-Country Nationals or TCNs) is common in the UAE's booming sectors. Understanding the process ensures your candidates arrive on time and fully compliant.

## Step 1: The Job Offer and Labor Market Test
Before applying for a work permit, employers often need to prove they couldn't find a suitable candidate from the EU/EEA. This usually involves registering the vacancy with NAFIS.

## Step 2: Application Submission via Identity the UAE
Once the labor market test is satisfied (or if the role is exempt), the employer submits an application for a Employment Visa through Identity the UAE's online portal.
- **Required Documents:** Include the employment contract, passport copies, health insurance, and a lease agreement.

## Step 3: Approval in Principle
If successful, Identity the UAE issues an "Approval in Principle" letter. The candidate uses this letter to apply for an entry visa at the nearest UAE embassy or consulate.

## Step 4: Arrival and Biometrics
Upon arriving in the UAE, the candidate must attend an appointment at Identity the UAE to capture biometrics and finalize the residence card issuance.
