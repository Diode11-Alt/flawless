# UAE Labor Law Gratuity Calculation Guide for 2026

End-of-service gratuity is a fundamental statutory right for expatriate employees in the United Arab Emirates under UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 on the Regulation of Labour Relations. Whether you are an HR executive managing payroll or a candidate evaluating an offer, understanding how gratuity is calculated in 2026 is essential.

## Key Gratuity Eligibility Rules

Under the current UAE labor law, all expatriate workers who have completed at least one full year of continuous service are entitled to end-of-service gratuity. Notably, the law eliminated the distinction between limited and unlimited contracts; all employment contracts are now structured as fixed-term contracts.

## Gratuity Calculation Formula

Gratuity is calculated based on the employee's **basic salary** (excluding allowances such as housing, transportation, and utilities) immediately preceding their termination or resignation:

1. **First 1 to 5 Years of Service:** You are entitled to **21 days of basic salary** for each year of service.
2. **Beyond 5 Years of Service:** You are entitled to **30 days of basic salary** for each additional year of service beyond 5 years.
3. **Maximum Limit:** The total gratuity payout cannot exceed the equivalent of **two years' total remuneration**.

### Formula Example for 3 Years of Service
If your monthly basic salary is AED 15,000 and you worked for 3 full years:
* Daily basic salary = AED 15,000 ÷ 30 = AED 500
* Gratuity per year = 21 days × AED 500 = AED 10,500
* Total Gratuity = AED 10,500 × 3 years = **AED 31,500**

## Pro-Rata Gratuity for Partial Years
If an employee terminates their contract after completing more than one year of continuous service, they are entitled to a pro-rated gratuity for any fraction of the final year worked, calculated on a daily basis.

## The UAE Savings Scheme Alternative (DEWS & Alternative Pension Systems)
In recent years, MOHRE and free zones like DIFC have introduced voluntary and mandatory workplace savings schemes (such as DEWS). Under these structured pension funds, employers contribute a fixed monthly percentage of the basic salary into a diversified investment fund, replacing standard end-of-service accruals with a modern wealth-building vehicle.
