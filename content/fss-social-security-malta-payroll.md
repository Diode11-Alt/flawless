# WPS & Social Security in the UAE: A Payroll Compliance Primer for Employers

Running a compliant payroll in the UAE requires a thorough understanding of the Final Settlement System (WPS) and Social Security contributions. 

## The Final Settlement System (WPS)
The WPS is the UAE's system for deducting income tax directly from employees' wages.
- **Employer Responsibility:** Employers must deduct tax at source based on the employee's tax status (single, married, or parent rates).
- **FS Forms:** Employers are required to submit monthly FS5 forms (payment of tax and national insurance) and annual FS3 (employee earnings) and FS7 (employer summary) forms to the Commissioner for Revenue (CfR).

## Social Security Contributions
Both employers and employees must contribute to the UAE Social Security system.
- **Rates:** Generally, the contribution is 10% of the employee's basic weekly wage from both the employer and the employee, capped at a specific maximum amount which is revised annually.
- **Benefits:** These contributions fund public healthcare, pensions, and unemployment benefits.
