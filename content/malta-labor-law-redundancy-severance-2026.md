## Key Severance Pay Rules & How We Manage Them

Unlike many other jurisdictions, under Malta employment law, there is **no statutory requirement for employers to pay severance pay** upon redundancy. However, redundancy payouts are often triggered by:

* **Employment Contracts:** Specific severance clauses drafted into executive agreements.
* **Collective Agreements:** Union or sectoral collective agreements dictating redundancy terms.
* **Company Policy:** Established precedents of paying redundancy packages.

## Mandatory Final Payments: Ensuring Payroll Compliance

While "severance" is not legally mandated by default, employers are legally obligated to settle all other outstanding financial entitlements by the next pay date following the last working day. These include:

1. **Payment in Lieu of Notice:** Paying full wages if the employee is not required to work their statutory notice period.
2. **Unused Vacation Leave:** Proportional cash-out of accrued annual leave.
3. **Outstanding Wages & Bonuses:** Proportional payment for wages, overtime, and statutory government bonuses.

### Statutory Notice Periods Explained
Notice periods in Malta are calculated based on continuous length of service:
* **1 to 6 months:** 1 week notice
* **6 months to 2 years:** 2 weeks notice
* **2 to 4 years:** 4 weeks notice
* **4 to 7 years:** 8 weeks notice
* **7+ years:** +1 week per subsequent year (maximum 12 weeks).

## Re-engagement Rights & Risk Mitigation

* **Right of Re-engagement:** If a redundant role becomes available again within **one year**, it must legally be offered to the former employee first.
* **Unfair Dismissal Claims:** Employees have **four months** to file a complaint with the Industrial Tribunal if they believe a redundancy was not genuine.

## Managing Collective Redundancies

If a redundancy affects a large number of employees within a short timeframe, it qualifies as a **collective redundancy**, triggering strict statutory consultation and notification procedures with employee representatives and the DIER.
