# DHA License Verification & Job Search Guide for Healthcare Professionals

Securing a medical or healthcare role in Dubai requires navigating strict regulatory frameworks to ensure patient safety and clinical excellence. For doctors, nurses, and allied health specialists, obtaining and verifying a Dubai Health Authority (DHA) license is the single most critical step in your career journey.

## Understanding the DHA Licensing Structure

The Dubai Health Authority regulates all healthcare professionals practicing within the Emirate of Dubai (excluding Dubai Healthcare City, which is governed by DHCC). Before an employer can onboard you, you must hold either a valid **DHA Eligibility Letter** or an active **DHA Professional License**.

### Key Phases of DHA License Verification
1. **Primary Source Verification (PSV):** Managed via DataFlow, this process independently verifies your educational degrees, professional licenses, and work experience certificates directly from the issuing institutions worldwide.
2. **CBT Assessment / Prometric Exam:** Depending on your clinical specialty and qualifications, you may be required to pass a computerized prometric examination administered in test centers globally.
3. **Issuance of Eligibility Letter:** Once your DataFlow PSV and exam results are cleared, DHA issues an Eligibility Letter valid for one year, allowing you to actively interview and accept job offers in Dubai.
4. **Facility Activation:** Upon signing an employment contract, your new employer's medical director transfers your eligibility letter to the hospital's facility license to issue your active practicing license.

## Top Tips for Healthcare Job Seekers in Dubai

* **Complete DataFlow Before Applying:** Employers prioritize candidates who already hold a DataFlow report or DHA Eligibility Letter, as it reduces recruitment onboarding time from months to weeks.
* **Keep Documents Normalized:** Ensure that the exact spelling of your name matches across your passport, medical degree, and certificate of good standing to avoid DataFlow rejection delays.
* **Understand License Portability:** Under recent UAE Ministry of Health rules, licenses are increasingly transferable between DHA (Dubai), DOH (Abu Dhabi), and MOHAP (Northern Emirates) without repeating exams, provided you meet experience prerequisites.
